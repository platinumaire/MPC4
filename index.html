The Core Insight

Why can't every day
be like the good days?

You've had days where the pain is manageable. Days where you almost feel normal. And days where getting out of bed feels impossible. What's the difference? Your pain isn't random.

☀️

Your good days might be

Patterns you haven't noticed yet

  • Sunny, above 50°, low humidity
  • During a new moon phase
  • After consistent sleep
  • Medication taken at the right time
  • Low geomagnetic activity
🌧️

Your bad days might cluster around

Triggers hiding in plain sight

  • Barometric pressure drops
  • Seasonal and weather shifts
  • Solar storm activity
  • Medication timing variance
  • Lunar cycle transitions

After a few weeks, real patterns emerge: "My pain is 30% higher when barometric pressure drops below 29.8" — Show your family, your friends, your doctors. This is why today was hard. This is what was happening.

Our Story

Built by someone
who gets it.

This app was built by someone who's been living with chronic pain for 11 years. Not a wellness entrepreneur. Not a tech bro with a "solution."

Someone who needed documentation for insurance denials, pharmacy refusals, and doctors who don't believe you. Because we know: pain affects your career, your relationships, your entire life.

MPC3 exists because there was no tool that treated chronic pain patients as intelligent adults who deserve data about their own bodies.

Women's pain is different.

Different medication dosages. Hormonal factors that don't show up in standard research. Cyclical patterns that matter. The medical field is finally acknowledging this. MPC3 tracks it all.

📋

Documentation That Advocates For You

Objective timestamped data to bring to doctor appointments. Counter "drug-seeking" assumptions with weeks of real patterns.

🧬

Genetic Metabolism Awareness

Rapid metabolizers process medications faster than 90% of the population. Standard dosing doesn't account for this. Your data can show what standard guidelines miss.

📊

Doctor-Ready Exports (Coming Soon)
